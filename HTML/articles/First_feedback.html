I received my first feedback from Paula Tavares Leitao Waaijenberg on 21st May 2024. It read the following text:

"Hello Maria,

Thank you for submitting your “Who Am I?” form. The feedback given here is for you to use when you start the program, so you don’t have to respond to it just now.

I read that your prior education was focused on Economic informatics, what have you learned so far? Did you already take our Programming Experience preparation assignment? https://learn.hz.nl/course/view.php?id=29015#section-5

Our program for the first year focuses mostly on programming (HTML, JavaScript, TypeScript, Laravel Framework) helping you not only to code but also to work as a professional team, with very hands-on approach working in some individual and many team project assignments.

From your SWOT analysis I read you are a quick learner and keep lots of information. Do you also perform well when the subject is less interesting? On the other hand, you say you are easily distracted. Does this also happen when the subject is interesting? Do you get bored if it becomes too easy? How do you cope working with a team?

You mention language level as a personal circumstance. But what is your English level? You must be able to follow the classes in this language, deliver well written reports, participate in team groups with a variety of nationalities, and take your exams in English. As described in the admission requirements, you need an equivalent to IELTS 6.0.

You also ask about the classes: there are not many hours of lessons, but the program includes a lot of self-study and hands-on working on project development. For someone with some programming experience, it might seem a bit easy in the first quartile, but the workload gets quite intense further on. With Programming Basic, for example, you will get access to a knowledge bank that you will need to study before the class. In the morning, during class we will explain the theory deeper and show some practical examples. Then you get some small assignments to work on, in dividually and in a group. In the afternoon we come back to a check-point where we discuss the assignments and answer further questions. In the meantime, you work on your own delivering weekly assignments, that get more and more complex each week.

HZ offers many extracurricular activities. Check https://hz.nl/en/study-at-hz/student-life. We also have our own ICT student association and they organise other events. This is their Instagram page: https://www.instagram.com/svhelloworld/

I am looking forward to meeting you upcoming year and wish you a lovely summer break!
Best regards,
Ana Paula Waaijenberg (ICT Lecturer)"
